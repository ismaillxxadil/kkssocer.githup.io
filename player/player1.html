Lionel Messi

Biography

Messi was born on June 24, 1987 south of Rosario, in the province of Santa Fe, Argentina, to Jorge Horacio Messi (born 1958), a factory worker, and Celia Maria Cuccitini, a cleaner, and his father's family is of Italian descent, specifically to the city of Ancona, from which his grandfather, Angelo Messi, emigrated in 1883. He has two older brothers, Rodrigo and Matías, as well as a sister, Maria Sol. At the age of five, Messi began playing football for a local club coached by his father Jorge called Grandoli. In 1995, Messi moved to Newell's Old Boys in Rosario, his hometown. In his eleventh spring, he discovered that he was deficient in growth hormones, and River Plate showed interest in signing Messi, but his management lacked sufficient funds to pay for his treatment, which amounted to $900 per month. Messi attracted the attention of Carlos Rexach, sporting director of FC Barcelona, who heard of his talent through a call from Messi's relatives in Lleida, Catalonia, so Messi's family got the opportunity to offer him to FC Barcelona, so he moved with his father to Spain, where he offered his talent to the club's management, and he stunned them to the point that they offered Messi's family to move to Spain in exchange for his treatment expenses. Based on this, the family moved to Europe and Messi began playing in the club's youth teams
